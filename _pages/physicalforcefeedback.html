--- layout: page title: Toys & Force Feedback permalink: /toys/ --- Physical force feedback consists of adding hardware to your virtual pinball cabinet — shaker motors, solenoids, blowing fans, chimes, gear motors, beacons, and strobes. In the virtual pinball community, we refer to these devices as **toys**. The purpose is to make the hardware react to the pinball table code in real time. For example, the shaker motor activates in Jurassic Park VPX when the dinosaur eats the ball, solenoids fire when the ball hits bumpers, and a real plunger can be used to launch the ball. --- ## Solenoids
Adding Solenoids

Add a 1N4007 diode on the positive terminal of each solenoid — this allows current to flow in only one direction and protects your controller board. Watch the video →

The grey bar of the diode connects to the positive terminal. The other side connects to the negative terminal.

Connections: Positive → 12V power supply. Negative → controller board port.

### Solenoid Recommendations A typical virtual pinball setup uses 10 solenoids: - **6** × 12V car/truck starter solenoids — for bumpers - **2** × Real pinball flipper assemblies - **2** × Real pinball slingshot assemblies - **1** × Real pinball knocker Car/truck solenoids work well on a 12V 20A supply. --- ## Shaker Motor
Adding a Shaker Motor

A shaker motor is one of the best additions to a cabinet. The Stern pinball shaker is the recommended option — it's been programmed into more than 300 virtual pinball tables.

Wiring any toy:

  • Add a 1N4007 diode if the device has a magnetic field (motor, solenoid)
  • Connect positive to the power supply
  • Add a fuse between negative and the controller port
  • Connect negative to a controller port
